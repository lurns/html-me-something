Top 5 Albums

  • The Con
  • Rumours
  • Deja Entendu
  • Building Nothing Out of Something
  • In Rainbows

The Con

Tegan and Sara

2007

It's hard to narrow down a favorite T&S album (and checking my old last.fm account shows If It Was You has more plays). Perhaps the bias is because The Con came out when I was about to start my senior year in high school and I have such a vivid memory of going to see the show at the Pageant with my best friend. Despite this album being more produced than their previous releases, it still feels nice and raw.

Rumours

Fleetwood Mac

1977

Continuing the theme of sad catchy beats, I have to give Fleetwood Mac an OG nod here. This album tells an incredible story (and yet still so relatable??), and how the band holds it together is beyond me.

Deja Entendu

Brand New

2003

The album (and band) that has stayed with me since my middle school years. This was another hard one to pick a favorite for, but each song has meant something to me during different times. It's funny to think of how deep I thought I was as a 13-year old.

Building Nothing Out of Something

Modest Mouse

1999

I liked a couple of Modest Mouse albums in high school, but this one hit me after the fact. And it came at a good time. It's comprised of other songs from other albums/ b-sides, so maybe that's why the sad theme works so well here.

In Rainbows

Radiohead

2007

This is the least blatant sad album of the list. It does have its sad songs, and I most cherished it during a sad time in my life. But for a Radiohead album, it's damn catchy. There are other Radiohead albums that I've listened to more, but this one has sentimental value.
